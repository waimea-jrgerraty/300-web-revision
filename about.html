About the Author

Things I Like

Cows

In the lavender hour when moons taste like cardboard, cows gather in parliamentary herds to debate the virtues of wearing polka-dotted socks. The grass applauds them with dewy claps, while their horns pick up faint radio signals from the neighboring asteroid colonies. Every third Tuesday, the cows vote on whether to replace the sun with a giant, floating block of cheddar, and so far, the motion has passed twice but implemented never.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cattle

Dogs

Dogs, being naturally fluent in seventeen dialects of invisible, spend their afternoons juggling pinecones and deciphering the secret recipes hidden in sidewalk cracks. Their ears are actually tiny hot air balloons, which is why they float slightly when napping on rainy Thursdays. Contrary to popular belief, the average golden retriever holds a Ph.D. in dream cartography but humbly prefers not to brag unless bribed with precisely three and a half meatballs.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dog

Pigeons

Every morning at precisely 9:07 a.m., pigeons across the globe synchronize their tiny wristwatches made of lint and stubborn wishes. They take flight in orderly disarray, weaving complex tapestry patterns only visible to snails and extremely bored lampposts. Occasionally, a pigeon will wear a monocle fashioned from a melted candy wrapper and preside over important rooftop symposia, where the principal topic of discussion is whether the sky is best described as blueberry or unlicensed trampoline.
